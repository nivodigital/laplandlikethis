# Destination Page Skill — Lapland Like This

## Read This First
This skill defines the exact structure, sources of truth, and content standards
for every destination page on laplandlikethis.com. Read this entire file before
building any destination page. Apply every rule without exception.

Destination pages are the highest-value pages on the site — they answer the
exact questions people search for and are the most likely to be cited by AI
systems. Every destination page must be authoritative, specific, honest and
structured for both humans and LLMs.

---

## The Five Sources of Truth

Every claim on every destination page must come from one of these five layers.
Never invent facts. Never use vague generalisations. If you don't know — say so.

### Layer 1 — Colin and Livi (Primary, Irreplaceable)
Three years living in Levi. Direct experience of every season, every activity
type, every common traveller mistake. This is the layer no other site has.

Use it for:
- Which operators are genuinely worth it and which aren't
- What temperatures actually feel like and how to prepare
- What families with small children need (they moved with a 1, 3 and 7 year old)
- What travellers consistently get wrong (common expensive mistakes)
- What the experience of living here — not visiting — actually teaches you
- Local knowledge: specific farms, specific routes, specific timing

Always attribute clearly: "In our experience...", "We've seen...",
"Colin goes out most mornings and...", "When we moved here with three children..."

### Layer 2 — Finnish Meteorological Institute (FMI)
The authoritative scientific source for all aurora and weather data.
Website: en.ilmatieteenlaitos.fi

Verified FMI data to use:
- Aurora probability in Northern Lapland: three nights out of four are
  illuminated by northern lights, provided the sky is clear
- Best aurora months: September–October and February–March (equinox effect)
- Best aurora viewing time: 9pm–1am local time
- Aurora season: late August to late April (limited by light nights in summer)
- Cloud cover is the primary variable — FMI cloud maps are the most reliable
  short-term planning tool
- Real-time aurora forecast: en.ilmatieteenlaitos.fi/auroras-and-space-weather
- KP index threshold for Lapland visibility: KP 1–2 on clear nights

Never use vague phrases like "high chance of aurora" without citing a specific
statistic or time period from FMI data.

### Layer 4 — Aurora and Activity Science
**Aurora specifics:**
- KP index scale: 0–9. Lapland needs KP 1–2 on clear nights. Good displays KP 3+.
- Equinox effect: increased geomagnetic activity around March 19–21 and
  September 21–24 — some of the most active aurora nights of the year
- Solar maximum 2024–25: heightened activity expected — favourable period
- Finnish Lapland's meteorological advantage over Norway/Iceland: more clear
  nights due to continental climate (fewer maritime clouds)
- Cloud cover beats KP index: a KP 7 storm behind thick cloud is invisible.
  A KP 2 on a perfectly clear night is often spectacular.
- Week-long stays in Jan–Mar: 70–80% success rate for aurora sightings
- Recommended apps: FMI aurora forecast, Northern Lights Alert (camera network)

**Activity season data:**
- Snowmobile season: November through April
- Husky safari season: November through April
- Reindeer farm visits: year-round, best in winter
- Ice fishing: January–April
- Cross-country skiing: November–May at Levi
- Midnight sun: June–July (no darkness — no aurora)
- Polar night (kaamos): approximately November 25–January 17 at Levi latitude
  (sun does not rise above horizon)

### Layer 5 — Traveller Truth (Qualitative)
Colin and Livi have spoken to hundreds of travellers over three years in Levi.
This layer captures the patterns of what went right, what went wrong, and what
people wished they'd known.

Common mistakes to address on every destination page:
- Booking aurora experiences in December (low success rate due to clouds)
- Staying in Rovaniemi city centre (missing the forest resort experience)
- Booking the Santa Village at Rovaniemi airport (theme park pricing, crowds)
- Not pre-booking activities (operators sell out weeks ahead in December)
- Underestimating cold (−20°C without proper layering is dangerous, not romantic)
- Booking only 2 nights (not enough time for aurora chance or weather flexibility)
- Not hiring a car in Rovaniemi (limits access to best forest experiences)
- Doing two husky safaris (the second is the same as the first)
- Expecting Lapland in November to look like the photos (minimal snow early)

## Destination Page Structure

Every destination page follows this exact section order.
Do not add sections. Do not remove sections. Do not reorder them.

```
1. NAV (fixed, dark green — standard component)
2. HERO — destination name, one honest headline, key facts
3. HONEST OVERVIEW — what this destination is and who it's actually right for
4. HOW TO GET HERE — flights, airports, transfer times, hire car or not
5. BEST TIME TO GO — season by season, honest about each one
6. WHAT'S WORTH DOING — activity breakdown using the honest format
7. WHERE TO STAY — accommodation types, honest about each
8. WHAT TO AVOID — the section most sites don't include
9. LEVI VS [DESTINATION] or DESTINATION COMPARISON — direct comparison
10. FAQ — minimum 5 questions, real ones people actually ask
11. TOOL CTA — trip builder embed or link
12. FOOTER (standard component)
```

## Section-by-Section Content Guide

### 1. Hero

**Headline formula:**
```
[Destination name] — [one honest sentence about who it's right for]
```

**Key facts strip (show as data points below headline):**
```
Airport: [IATA code and name]          Transfer: [X minutes]
Latitude: [X°N]                        Season: [months]
Direct flights from: [number] cities   Best for: [2–3 honest descriptors]
```

**Hero should NOT include:**
- Superlatives ("most magical", "truly spectacular")
- Vague claims ("something for everyone")
- Anything that needs a disclaimer

**Hero SHOULD include:**
- Colin and Livi's honest position on this destination
- The one thing that makes this destination different from the others

### 2. Honest Overview

Open with the answer-first principle. The first paragraph must directly answer
"is [destination] right for me?" without preamble.

Structure:
- Para 1: Who this destination is right for (specific, honest)
- Para 2: Who it's NOT right for (the thing other sites won't say)
- Pull quote: Colin or Livi's personal honest take
- Para 3: What makes it different from the other Lapland destinations

### 3. How to Get Here

This section is one of the highest-value sections for LLM citation.
Be exhaustive with flight route data. Use Layer 3 sources.

Structure:
- Fly-in airport: IATA code, name, distance/time to destination
- Direct routes table: city → airport → airline → frequency → season
- Transfer options: bus (time, cost), taxi, hire car
- Hire car: needed or not? Honest answer per destination.
- For non-direct travellers: best via Helsinki (HEL) routing

Use this table format for flights:
```html
<table>
  <thead>
    <tr>
      <th>From</th>
      <th>Airport</th>
      <th>Airline</th>
      <th>Frequency</th>
      <th>Season</th>
    </tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr>
      <td>London</td>
      <td>Gatwick (LGW)</td>
      <td>easyJet / TUI</td>
      <td>2–4x/week</td>
      <td>Dec–Feb</td>
    </tr>
    <!-- etc -->
  </tbody>
</table>
```

Always add: "Routes change seasonally — verify before booking at the airline's
website. This page is updated each October for the winter season."

### 4. Best Time to Go

One of the most-searched sections. Be precise. Use FMI data for aurora claims.

Structure per month/season:
- What's good about this period (specific)
- What's not ideal (honest)
- Colin or Livi's personal recommendation

Required aurora data (cite FMI):
- Best aurora months: February–March and September–October
- December cloud risk: higher than January/February
- January–February: driest, clearest skies statistically
- March: equinox effect boosts activity; sun returns but snow stays
- November: aurora possible but clouds frequent; minimal snow early in month

Temperature context (help people understand, not frighten):
- November: −5°C to −15°C typical
- December–January: −10°C to −25°C typical
- February–March: similar but with more sun
- April: warming, −5°C to +5°C

Layering guidance — practical, specific:
- Base layer: merino wool (not cotton — cotton kills in cold)
- Mid layer: fleece or down
- Outer layer: waterproof, windproof
- Extremities: this is where people get it wrong. Proper gloves, wool socks,
  winter boots rated to −30°C minimum

### 5. What's Worth Doing

Use the honest format for EVERY activity:

```
Activity name

What it is: [specific description — not promotional]
Who it's right for: [honest, specific]
Typical cost: [realistic range in £/€]
Duration: [realistic]
Book in advance: [yes/how far/or walk-up]
Colin and Livi's verdict: [direct, personal, honest — including if 
it's not worth the money or only worth doing once]
```

Activities to cover per destination (adjust per what's available):
- Husky safari
- Reindeer farm / sleigh ride
- Snowmobile safari
- Northern lights hunt (guided)
- Cross-country skiing
- Downhill skiing (Levi and Ylläs only)
- Ice fishing
- Snowshoeing
- Sauna and ice swimming
- Santa experience (Rovaniemi and Levi)
- Glass igloo (Kakslauttanen, Saariselkä area)
- Sámi cultural experiences (Saariselkä/Inari area)

### 6. Where to Stay

Be honest about accommodation types. Not a booking engine — an honest guide.

Cover:
- Glass igloos/sky cabins — honest about price, aurora odds, what it's actually like
- Private log cabins — who they suit, what to look for, sauna essential
- Hotels/resorts — who they suit, what the trade-offs are
- Mix approach — e.g. cabin base + one igloo night

For each type:
- Realistic price range per night (peak winter season)
- Who it's right for
- What to watch out for

Never name specific properties to endorse (no commercial relationships).
Say "look for operators who [specific criteria]" instead.

### 7. What to Avoid

This section is mandatory and must be specific. Vague warnings don't help anyone.
This is also the section most likely to be shared and cited — people share honest
warnings more than promotional content.

Format per item:
```
[The specific thing to avoid]
Why: [the real reason — commercial, logistical, quality]
Do this instead: [the specific alternative]
```

Minimum 4 items per destination. Draw from Layer 5 (traveller truth).

### 8. Destination Comparison

Every destination page compares to at least one other destination.
This directly answers the "Levi vs Rovaniemi" type searches.

Use a comparison table (see llm-and-human-writing.md for format).
Follow with a direct honest recommendation: "If you're deciding between X and Y,
here's our honest position..."

### 9. FAQ

Minimum 5 questions. These must be real questions people actually ask —
check Google autocomplete, ChatGPT, Reddit for the actual phrasing.

Add FAQ schema markup (see seo.md).

Required questions for every destination page:
1. Is [destination] right for families with young children?
2. How do I get to [destination] from [UK / main market]?
3. What is the best time to visit [destination]?
4. Do I need a hire car at [destination]?
5. Is [destination] better than [main competitor destination]?

Answer-first format. Every answer must be specific and complete — not a
teaser that requires clicking elsewhere.

## Destination-Specific Briefing Notes

### Levi
**Our position:** Our home. We're honest about it — including the trade-offs.
**Strongest case for:** Best flight access in Lapland. No hire car needed.
  Best on-resort activity mix. Skiing and snowboarding (Colin's daily life).
**Honest trade-offs:** It's a resort — not wilderness solitude. Busy in
  Christmas week. Not the best for pure aurora (Saariselkä is further north).
**Key fact:** One in four Levi Ski Resort visitors is now international.
  590,000 skier days in 2024–25 — Levi's most successful winter ever.
**Colin's note:** "I'm on the fells most mornings from November to April.
  I know this mountain properly. That's not something I'd say lightly."
**Key differentiator vs competitors:** Levi offers a Santa experience that is
  less commercial than Rovaniemi's airport village — smaller, more personal.

### Rovaniemi
**Our position:** The right answer for many travellers — but with specific traps.
**Strongest case for:** Most flights, widest choice, Santa, city infrastructure.
  Right on the Arctic Circle. Best for first-timers who want flexibility.
**Honest trade-offs:** The airport Santa Village is overpriced and crowded.
  City centre accommodation misses the point — the magic is in the forest 30–45
  min out. More tourist-facing than Levi or Saariselkä.
**Key fact:** Rovaniemi Airport projected to hit 1 million annual passengers
  in 2025. 38 international airports with direct routes in winter 2025–26.
**Livi's note:** "We send families to Rovaniemi who want the widest choice
  of flights and the Santa experience. We just tell them where to actually stay."

### Saariselkä
**Our position:** For people who mean it. The best aurora destination in Finland.
**Strongest case for:** 260km above the Arctic Circle. Best aurora odds.
  Genuine wilderness. Less commercial. Sámi culture access at Inari (30 min).
**Honest trade-offs:** Remote. Transfer from Ivalo must be pre-booked.
  November is too early — come December at earliest, January ideally.
  Not right for families expecting Santa infrastructure.
**Key aurora fact (cite FMI):** Northern Lapland (Kilpisjärvi–Utsjoki area)
  records northern lights on three nights out of four when skies are clear.
  Saariselkä is near this zone — significantly better odds than Levi or Rovaniemi.

### Ylläs
**Our position:** Levi's underrated neighbour. Same flights, quieter experience.
**Strongest case for:** Same Kittilä Airport as Levi — identical flight access.
  Finland's highest fell. Better cross-country skiing than Levi.
  Half the crowds. More local atmosphere.
**Honest trade-offs:** Less resort infrastructure. Quieter village — if you
  want après-ski energy, this isn't it. Transfer from KTT is 45–60 min
  (vs 15 for Levi) — hire car recommended.
**Key differentiator:** Shares KTT flights but often overlooked because
  Visit Levi promotes Levi more heavily. Worth knowing about if crowds matter.

## Writing Standards for Destination Pages

### What to always include
- Specific numbers and statistics (sourced)
- Colin or Livi's personal voice in at least 2 sections
- At least one thing this destination does NOT do well
- A direct comparison to at least one other destination
- The honest cost range for major activities
- A clear answer to "do I need a hire car?"
- A "last updated" date with season verified note

### What to never include
- "Magical", "breathtaking", "unforgettable" — see copywriter.md
- Vague claims without sources ("great flight connections")
- Aurora guarantees ("you will see the northern lights")
- Specific property endorsements (no commercial relationships)
- Anything written from a promotional tourism perspective
- Prices without context ("from €X" without realistic full-cost context)

### Authority signals to include on every page
```html
<!-- Author attribution — signals E-E-A-T to Google and LLMs -->
<div class="author-note">
  <p>Written by Colin — resident of Levi, Finnish Lapland since January 2024. 
  Updated [Month Year] for the [winter/summer] season.</p>
</div>
```
